Health Practitioner Regulation National Law - via Ahpra

This law ensures that only qualified and registered professionals can provide healthcare in Australia. It is managed by the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (Ahpra). For dental practices, this includes dentists, dental hygienists, and oral health therapists. Dental assistants must also work within the supervision and scope of these registered professionals.

2. Poisons and Therapeutic Goods Act 2001 - NSW

Why it matters in dentistry: Dental assistants may handle or assist with medications and therapeutic goods. It’s important to understand what substances you can legally manage and how to do so safely, especially in terms of storage, labelling, and disposal.

+

3. Health Records and Information Privacy Act 2002 - NSW

Why it matters: As a dental assistant, you may handle patient records, forms, or digital systems. You must never share a patient’s personal information without permission and must follow strict privacy procedures to avoid legal breaches.

+

4. Radiation Control Act - NSW

Why it matters in a dental practice: Even if you are not operating the X-ray machine, you need to follow safety procedures like standing behind barriers, wearing PPE, and ensuring patients are shielded. Only trained staff can perform radiographic procedures, and safety zones must be respected.
